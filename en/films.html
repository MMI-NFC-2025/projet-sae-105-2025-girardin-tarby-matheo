Film gallery

Lise Meitner : The Pioneer of the Atom Bomb, by Wolf von Truchsess

Directed by Wolf von Truchsess, this documentary returns to the central role of Lise Meitner in the discovery of nuclear fission. He questions his historical erasure and highlights his refusal to participate in the manufacture of the atomic bomb.

Les figures de l’ombre, by Theodore Melfi

This film traces the journey of three African-American NASA mathematicians - Katherine Johnson, Dorothy Vaughan and Mary Jackson - whose calculations were essential to the success of American space missions. A powerful work that revealed their long-ignored role.

Radioactive, by Marjane Satrapi

This biopic highlights the life of Marie Curie, a two-time Nobel laureate and pioneer in the study of radioactivity. The film explores her discoveries, her personal struggles and the resistances she faced as a woman in a masculine scientific world.

Jane, by Brett Morgen

This documentary traces the life of Jane Goodall, a British primatologist and ethologist, whose work on chimpanzees revolutionized behavioral biology. The film celebrates its innovative approach and commitment to nature.
